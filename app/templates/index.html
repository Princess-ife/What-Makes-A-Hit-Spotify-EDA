The 4 Popularity Tiers

Spotify popularity scores range from 0 to 100. Here is what each tier means in the real world.

Viral

80-100

The top 1% of all songs on Spotify. These tracks are the most streamed and widely recognized, often dominating charts and playlists. And only 953 songs in the dataset reached this tier.

Hit

50-79

These songs are well-known and frequently streamed, often featured in playlists and radio rotations. They have a strong fanbase and consistent streaming numbers.

Mid

20-49

These tracks have dedicated listeners and moderate streaming numbers. But they haven't broken into mainstream popularity yet. They are often strong within a specific genre or reginonal market.

Flop

0-19

These tracks have low streaming numbers and limited recognition. 14% of the songs never find an audience. And once a song lands here, it almost never recovers.

Understanding Your Audio Features

These are the 10 audio features the model uses to analyse your song. You can find these values for any exisiting song on Tunebat.com.

Danceability

How easy is it to dance to this song? It is measured on a scale from 0 to 1. 0 means completely undanceable, 1 means built for the dance floor.

Energy

How energetic is the song? It is measured on a scale from 0 to 1. 0 means completely calm, 1 means extremely high energy.

Speechiness

How much does the song sound like talking? 0 means no speech, 1 means only speech.

Acousticness

How acoustic is the song? 0 means not acoustic at all, 1 means completely acoustic.

Instrumentalness

How instrumental is the song? 0 means it has vocals, 1 means it is purely instrumental.

Loudness

How loud is the song? This is measured in decibels (dB), it typically ranges from -60 dB (quiet) to 0 dB (loud).

Tempo

How fast is the song? It is measured in beats per minute (BPM), it indicates the speed of the track.

Duration

How long is the song? The sweet spot for Spotify hits is between 2.5 and 4 minutes.

Genre

What genre does the song belong to? Genre is the single strongest predictor of popularity in this model

Valence

How happy or sad does the song sound? 0 is dark and melancholic, 1 is cheerful and euphoric.

What This Predictor Does and Doesn't Do

What It Does

  • Provides a data-driven prediction of your song's popularity tier based on its audio features.
  • Offers insights into which features are most influential in determining popularity.
  • Helps you understand how your song compares to existing hits and flops.

What It Doesn't Do

  • It doesn't guarantee that your song will be a hit or a flop. It's a prediction based on patterns in the data.
  • It doesn't account for external factors like marketing, timing, viral moments driven by Tiktok or cultural trends that can influence a song's success.
  • It doesn't measure the artistic quality or emotional impact of your song, which are also important for success.
